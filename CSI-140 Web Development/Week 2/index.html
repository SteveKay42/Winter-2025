Crochet Stitches

Crochet is an art that is wide-ranging in its applications. You can make toys, clothing, blankets, housewares, among many more.


In crochet, there are a variety of stitches you can use to create your unique projects. They range from the basic to the complicated. Below are a few of the most common!


Basic Stitches

  1. Chain Stitch (ch): Foundation of most projects.
  2. Single Crochet (sc): Simple and dense stitch.
  3. Half Double Crochet (hdc): Between single and double crochet in height.
  4. Double Crochet (dc): Taller and looser stitch.

Intermediate Stitches

  • Treble (Triple) Crochet (tr): Even taller than double crochet.
  • Double Treble Crochet (dtr): Extremely tall stitch.
  • Cluster Stitches: Group of stitches worked together.

Advanced Stitches

  • Cables: Mimic the look of knitted cables.
  • Popcorn, Bobbles, and Puffs: Create textured effects.
  • Shell Stitches: Fan-like clusters of stitches.

Specialty Stitches

  • Tunisian Crochet: Uses a longer hook and creates a fabric that looks woven.
  • Broomstick Lace: Involves wrapping stitches around a broomstick or large knitting needle.
  • Hairpin Lace: Uses a hairpin lace loom and creates an airy, lacy effect.

Decorative Stitches

  • Picots: Small loops used for edging.
  • Crab Stitch (Reverse Single Crochet): Stitched backwards for a unique edge.
  • Crocodile Stitch: Scales-like pattern, great for dragon-inspired designs.

Joining Stitches

  • Slip Stitch (sl st): Used for joining or moving stitches without adding height.
  • Whip Stitch: Sewing stitch used to join pieces together.
